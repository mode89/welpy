1 · Collapse the reflow pipeline into one seam

Strong in-process found by 2 explorations

commands.py:140–225 · input.py:268,289 · output.py:25–37 · geometry.apply_hierarchy / apply_visibility / apply_tree / reconcile

Before · ordering re-typed at 7 sites

After · one deep entry point

Problem. The reflow ordering (tree before geometry before focus before publish) is an invariant that lives nowhere — only in each caller's line order, retyped at 7 sites.

Solution. One geometry.reflow(server, monitor=None) owns the sequence; callers make one call.

  • locality: ordering lives in one place
  • leverage: one interface, 7 call sites
  • interface shrinks from 6 ordered calls to 1
  • becomes a clean override point
  • tests assert one reflow, not 6 stubs
  • kills the per-monitor loop copy-paste
Keep the split points. The five apply_*/reconcile functions stay individually overridable (AGENTS.md). Only the ordering stops being an exported contract — reflow wraps them, it doesn't absorb them.

2 · A polymorphic window interface for the xdg/X11 fork

Strong local-substitutable found by 2 explorations

geometry.py:189,202,213,240,410,418,425,433 · commands.py:117 · windows.py:205 · input.py:309 · focus.py:216

Before · kind re-discriminated at 12 sites

After · fork lives behind one interface

Problem. A window's per-kind behavior is real, but it crosses every call seam as a repeated isinstance switch — 12 sites re-discriminate the kind.

Solution. Hang the ~6 polymorphic operations on XdgClient/X11Client; callers see one interface.

  • locality: kind-knowledge in one place
  • adding a window kind = one module
  • delete the isinstance ladder
  • leverage: uniform interface, 12 sites
  • xdg/X11 duality testable per kind
  • deletion test: divergence reappears at 12 sites
Override-model nuance. Methods stay overridable (AGENTS.md treats methods as patchable), so a config can still swap per-kind behavior. geometry.configure_x11 currently stays module-level for cross-module callers (MEMORY) — weigh method-on-Client vs. a dispatch table during grilling.

4 · Split focus target-selection from effect emission

Worth exploring in-process

focus.py: reconcile (26–117, disable=too-many-branches)

Before · policy + effects fused

After · pure selection seam

Problem. "Which surface gets the keyboard" is interleaved with the cffi convergence effects, so the policy can't be tested or overridden without the effects firing.

Solution. Extract focus_target(server) → (surface, client, ls); reconcile selects then emits.

  • policy unit-tested without cffi
  • config overrides target rule alone
  • dissolves the too-many-branches disable
  • leverage: one rule, one seam

Top recommendation

Collapse the reflow pipeline (candidate 1)

Highest leverage, smallest change. Two independent explorations landed on it: the reflow ordering is an invariant retyped at 7 sites with nothing enforcing it. One geometry.reflow seam concentrates the ordering (locality), serves every caller (leverage), and becomes a clean override point — without touching the five split points configs already patch.

Then candidate 2 (polymorphic window interface): more files, more behavior, but the deletion test is unambiguous — the xdg/X11 divergence reappears at 12 sites today.
